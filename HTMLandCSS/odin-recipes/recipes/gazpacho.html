Spanish Gazpacho Soup

Description

Gazpacho is a classic cold soup originating from Spain, known for its refreshing taste and vibrant colors. Made from ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, onions, and a touch of garlic, this dish is blended with extra-virgin olive oil and sherry vinegar to create a smooth and tangy flavor.

Traditionally served chilled, gazpacho is perfect for hot summer days and can be garnished with reserved vegetables for added texture and presentation. The balance of fresh ingredients and simple preparation makes gazpacho a delicious and healthy option, ideal for light meals or as a starter.

Ingredients

  • 6 large ripe beefsteak tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 large cucumber, chopped
  • 1 medium green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 medium yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 large sweet onion, chopped
  • 1 small jalapeno pepper, seeded and diced, or to taste
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons sherry vinegar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Set aside a few pieces of tomato, cucumber, and bell peppers for garnish.
  2. Combine remaining tomatoes, cucumber, and bell peppers with onion, jalapeno, and garlic in a food processor; process until all vegetables are finely chopped. Mix in olive oil, sherry vinegar, and salt.
  3. Cover and refrigerate until chilled, at least 1 hour.
  4. Season with pepper before serving and garnish with reserved tomatoes, cucumbers, and bell peppers.
